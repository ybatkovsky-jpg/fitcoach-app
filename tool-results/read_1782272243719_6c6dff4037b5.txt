    23вҶ’  4572вҶ’Specificity, Overload, and Progression
    24вҶ’  4573вҶ’A resistance training program is planned around the needs, goals, and current
    25вҶ’  4574вҶ’condition of the clients. Effective, long-term adaptation to a resistance training
    26вҶ’  4575вҶ’stimulus is guided by key principles that allow the body to adapt to the stress
    27вҶ’  4576вҶ’response from resistance training. This stress response can be created by a
    28вҶ’  4577вҶ’manipulation of the three primary principles of resistance training. These
    29вҶ’  4578вҶ’three principles are specificity, overload, and progression. The goal of exercise
    30вҶ’  4579вҶ’program design is to manipulate the variables of specificity, overload, and
    31вҶ’  4580вҶ’progression for a desired outcome. This creates a training overload and causes
    32вҶ’  4581вҶ’physical adaptation.

    38вҶ’  4587вҶ’Principle of Specificity
    39вҶ’  4588вҶ’Our body adapts to the specific demands imposed on it. This principle of specificity is critical to understand! The specific adaptation to imposed demands
    40вҶ’  4589вҶ’(SAID) principle of training means that, for example, when a client works with
    41вҶ’  4590вҶ’barbells, their body will adapt to that stimulus, then when the same exercises
    42вҶ’  4591вҶ’are performed with dumbbells, they will readapt. Each change of equipment
    43вҶ’  4592вҶ’is a different stimulus and each new exercise provides a different training
    44вҶ’  4593вҶ’overload, even when using the same muscle group.
    45вҶ’  4594вҶ’This also means that when transitioning new clients from machine-based
    46вҶ’  4595вҶ’training into a small group with different equipment, there will be a whole new
    47вҶ’  4596вҶ’set of adaptation challenges. Balance, coordination, body control, and stabilization are new constraints that the individual needs to develop in order to
    48вҶ’  4597вҶ’perform skills correctly. For a client whoвҖҷs been training on a machine, the pads
    49вҶ’  4598вҶ’of the machine stabilize and hold the body in place, the arms of the machine
    50вҶ’  4599вҶ’guide the limbs, and although strength is built, less control, stabilization, or
    51вҶ’  4600вҶ’balance is involved. Transitioning the client to standing exercises with elastic
    52вҶ’  4601вҶ’resistance bands means that loads need to be lighter, communication needs to
    53вҶ’  4602вҶ’be clear and concise, and exercise progressions and regressions become critical.
    54вҶ’  4603вҶ’The beauty of the specificity principle is that when training with simple exercises that represent functional movements found in daily life, such as squats,
    55вҶ’  4604вҶ’lunges, pushing, pulling, and rotational movements, an improvement in elements like coordination, balance, and body control is seen, thereby enhancing
    56вҶ’  4605вҶ’sports performance or activities of daily life. Clients who train functionally
    57вҶ’  4606вҶ’will naturally develop more coordination, body control, and balance, leading
    58вҶ’  4607вҶ’to greater movement efficiency.

    60вҶ’  4609вҶ’Principle of Overload
    61вҶ’  4610вҶ’To produce desired adaptations to a training program, the variables of the exercise program must be manipulated to create an overload on the physiological
    62вҶ’  4611вҶ’systems of the body. Overload can be achieved by increasing the load, increasing the volume (number of reps or sets), or decreasing rest between exercises
    63вҶ’  4612вҶ’or sets. In other words, for training to be effective, itвҖҷs necessary to drive the
    64вҶ’  4613вҶ’body to work harder than what itвҖҷs used to doing. The level of overload must
    65вҶ’  4614вҶ’be appropriate to the training experience and fitness level of the clients. When
    66вҶ’  4615вҶ’this principle is not correctly applied, injuries are more likely to occur. Muscles
    67вҶ’  4616вҶ’gain strength more rapidly than tendons and ligaments, so appropriate loading
    68вҶ’  4617вҶ’is an important consideration. For deconditioned and inexperienced clients,
    69вҶ’  4618вҶ’less intensity can provide adequate overload.
    70вҶ’  4619вҶ’Volume is defined as the total amount of work performed (weight lifted)
    71вҶ’  4620вҶ’during an exercise session. It is generally expressed as a product of intensity Г—
    72вҶ’  4621вҶ’sets Г— number of repetitions. The total volume of a workout must be dictated
    73вҶ’  4622вҶ’by training experience and goals of the clients. Volume increases time under
    74вҶ’  4623вҶ’tension (TUT), which provides the training stimulus for muscular hypertrophy.
    75вҶ’  4624вҶ’Greater volume also requires greater amounts of time and should therefore be
    76вҶ’  4625вҶ’considered when planning your classes. Most critical is the fact that high-volume
    77вҶ’  4626вҶ’training is a natural progression and is unsuitable for novice exercisers. When
    78вҶ’  4627вҶ’training deconditioned individuals, lower workout volumes are more appropriate.
    79вҶ’  4628вҶ’Intensity is defined as the specific amount of resistance or external load
    80вҶ’  4629вҶ’applied to muscles. If the same resistive force is consistently applied, then

   166вҶ’  4715вҶ’Periodization
   167вҶ’  4716вҶ’Resistance training programs consist of numerous variables, including load
   168вҶ’  4717вҶ’(intensity), volume (reps and sets), frequency, rest interval, exercise selection,
   169вҶ’  4718вҶ’and exercise order (Williams et al., 2017). Periodization is defined as planned,
   170вҶ’  4719вҶ’cyclical (i.e., the program repeats) manipulation of these resistance training
   171вҶ’  4720вҶ’variables to attain peak performance at specific times of the year (Evans, 2019).
   172вҶ’  4721вҶ’In small-group training, an important consideration may be that load is not
   173вҶ’  4722вҶ’always a variable that can easily be manipulated. Bodyweight exercises and
   174вҶ’  4723вҶ’commonly used equipment such as rubber resistance bands and suspension
   175вҶ’  4724вҶ’training systems limit how much load can be added; therefore other variables
   176вҶ’  4725вҶ’(volume, rest interval, exercise selection, and exercise order) may need to be
   177вҶ’  4726вҶ’adjusted for SGT programming when considering the objectives of the group.
   178вҶ’  4727вҶ’Periodization training originated in sports with an annual competitive season,
   179вҶ’  4728вҶ’which require different levels of training volume, intensity, and specificity;
   180вҶ’  4729вҶ’these variables are manipulated depending on the competition schedule and
   181вҶ’  4730вҶ’the athleteвҖҷs goal. The method was developed to prevent overtraining, help prevent plateaus, and build optimal performance and fitness for the competitive
   182вҶ’  4731вҶ’season. Evans (2019) cites research indicating that lengthy periods of training
   183вҶ’  4732вҶ’that lack variation results in stagnation and fatigue. Consequently, one of the
   184вҶ’  4733вҶ’main purposes of periodization is to provide structure to the variability in
   185вҶ’  4734вҶ’the training components to decrease the likelihood of a plateau or decline in
   186вҶ’  4735вҶ’physical (and mental) performance. The success of periodization in sports has
   187вҶ’  4736вҶ’brought the protocol into mainstream fitness settings and with smart planning,
   188вҶ’  4737вҶ’can be incorporated into SGT settings.

   484вҶ’  5033вҶ’THE LAW OF FACILITATION
   485вҶ’  5034вҶ’When examining the human movement system, there is one additional element to
   486вҶ’  5035вҶ’consider: the law of facilitation. This principle is based on the idea that the human
   487вҶ’  5036вҶ’body will facilitate movement along the path of least resistance and will move habitually through the same path of motion, even when there is a lack of movement
   488вҶ’  5037вҶ’efficiency. These compensatory movement patterns become so ingrained that the
   489вҶ’  5038вҶ’individual may not be conscious of the habit. Think of someone walking with a limp.
   490вҶ’  5039вҶ’Without being aware, they favor one side of the body and their gait changes to accommodate pain and the movement pattern then becomes habitual.
   491вҶ’  5040вҶ’ItвҖҷs also important to consider how this affects the stabilityвҖ“mobility relationships
   492вҶ’  5041вҶ’of the body. When a mobile joint lacks mobility, the body will facilitate that movement from the stable structure above or below in another plane of motion. This further disrupts the motor program, and smooth, coordinated, efficient motor patterns
   493вҶ’  5042вҶ’develop into movement compensations. For example, a knee problem is frequently
   494вҶ’  5043вҶ’caused by an ankleвҖҷs lack of mobility, forcing the knee to move into other planes of
   495вҶ’  5044вҶ’motion. ItвҖҷs easy to look at the symptom and not the cause, but by understanding
   496вҶ’  5045вҶ’the relationships between stability and mobility, you can begin to unlock the secrets
   497вҶ’  5046вҶ’behind the causes of many of the bodyвҖҷs compensatory tendencies.
   498вҶ’  5047вҶ’Considering these relationships is important for exercise selection, program design, and injury prevention. Although the individual goals of group training make
   499вҶ’  5048вҶ’this more challenging, all human bodies function similarly. If you run a corporate
   500вҶ’  5049вҶ’wellness program, for example, you may have weight loss, stress reduction, and
   501вҶ’  5050вҶ’strength gain as individual goals for each client, but most of them sit for a living and
   502вҶ’  5051вҶ’will therefore have related stability and mobility issues.
