   297‚Üí   346‚Üí   370‚Üí   370‚ÜíMedicine Ball Training
   298‚Üí   347‚Üí   371‚Üí   371‚ÜíMedicine balls can be traced back to ancient Persia, Greece, and Rome, where
   299‚Üí   348‚Üí   372‚Üí   372‚Üíthey were used by athletes to increase strength, power, and athletic performance.
   300‚Üí   349‚Üí   373‚Üí   373‚ÜíIt is believed that the ancient Greeks stuffed animal skins for patients to toss for
   301‚Üí   350‚Üí   374‚Üí   374‚Üímedicinal purposes. A similar type of ball is believed to have been used in Persia
   302‚Üí   351‚Üí   375‚Üí   375‚Üíin the 1700s. The term medicine ball can be dated back to 1876 (Roberts 2020). Hip-
   303‚Üí   352‚Üí   376‚Üí   376‚Üípocrates believed them to be an essential tool for helping people regain normal body
   304‚Üí   353‚Üí   377‚Üí   377‚Üímovement after injury, as well as a means for enhancing and maintaining overall
   305‚Üí   354‚Üí   378‚Üí   378‚Üíhealth. Few, if any, pieces of equipment are versatile in the way that medicine
   306‚Üí   355‚Üí   379‚Üí   379‚Üíballs are: not only can they be lifted, but they can also be thrown and slammed.
   307‚Üí   356‚Üí   380‚Üí   380‚ÜíThe adaptability of medicine balls enables users to utilize nontraditional methods
   308‚Üí   357‚Üí   381‚Üí   381‚Üíto expand training goals and objectives. In addition, nearly every medicine ball
   309‚Üí   358‚Üí   382‚Üí   382‚Üíexercise can be combined with other exercises to create a continuous flow with
   310‚Üí   359‚Üí   383‚Üí   383‚Üíminimal rest. This is beneficial for two reasons:
   311‚Üí   360‚Üí   384‚Üí   384‚Üí1. It mimics functional movements used in everyday life.
   312‚Üí   361‚Üí   385‚Üí   385‚Üí2. It enables users to program any number of exercise combinations and varia-
   313‚Üí   362‚Üí   386‚Üí   386‚Üítions.
   314‚Üí   363‚Üí   387‚Üí   387‚ÜíToday, medicine balls are used to develop power, strength, anaerobic endurance,
   315‚Üí   364‚Üí   388‚Üí   388‚Üíand cardiorespiratory fitness.
   316‚Üí   365‚Üí   389‚Üí   389‚ÜíMedicine balls can be soft or firm, can come with or without handles, and can
   317‚Üí   366‚Üí   390‚Üí   390‚Üívary in weight from 2 to 100 pounds (0.9 to 45.4 kilograms). Medicine ball training
   318‚Üí   367‚Üí   391‚Üí   391‚Üíhas a wide spectrum of applications, ranging from injury rehabilitation to strength
   319‚Üí   368‚Üí   392‚Üí   392‚Üíand sports-performance enhancement (Faigenbaum et al. 2006; Hagberg et al.
   320‚Üí   369‚Üí   393‚Üí   393‚Üí2009). Its ability to recruit a significant number of muscle groups and to develop
   321‚Üí   370‚Üí   394‚Üí   394‚Üíexplosive power through functional movement makes medicine ball training an
   322‚Üí   371‚Üí   395‚Üí   395‚Üíessential tool in fitness, rehabilitation, and sports performance (Vincent, Traywick,
   323‚Üí   372‚Üí   396‚Üí   396‚Üíand Washburn 2013). When training with a medicine ball, perform 10 to 15 reps
   324‚Üí   373‚Üí   397‚Üí   397‚Üíor perform reps for a designated time, provided that you can do so with good form
   325‚Üí   374‚Üí   398‚Üí   398‚Üí(Faigenbaum et al. 2006). When selecting a medicine ball, it is important to choose
   326‚Üí   375‚Üí   399‚Üí   399‚Üíone heavy enough to reduce movement speed but not so heavy that technique,
   327‚Üí   376‚Üí   400‚Üí   400‚Üíform, and range of motion will be negatively impacted. Medicine balls weighing
   328‚Üí   377‚Üí   401‚Üí   401‚Üíbetween 4 and 15 pounds (1.8 to 6.8 kilograms) are generally recommended as a
   329‚Üí   378‚Üí   402‚Üí   402‚Üístarting point (Faigenbaum et al. 2006; Vincent, Traywick, and Washburn 2013).
   330‚Üí   379‚Üí   403‚Üí   403‚ÜíMedicine ball training can be effective as a fat-burning exercise when periods of

   478‚Üí   527‚Üí   551‚Üí   551‚ÜíComponents of Periodization
   479‚Üí   528‚Üí   552‚Üí   552‚ÜíThere are three concepts central to understanding periodization: general adap-
   480‚Üí   529‚Üí   553‚Üí   553‚Üítation syndrome, stimulus‚Äìfatigue‚Äìrecovery‚Äìadaptation (SFRA) theory, and the
   481‚Üí   530‚Üí   554‚Üí   554‚Üífitness‚Äìfatigue paradigm. In the following, we explain what they are and how
   482‚Üí   531‚Üí   555‚Üí   555‚Üíthey fit into metabolic training.
   483‚Üí   532‚Üí   556‚Üí   556‚ÜíGeneral Adaptation Syndrome
   484‚Üí   533‚Üí   557‚Üí   557‚ÜíThis theory is the most fundamental to physical training. When you perform a
   485‚Üí   534‚Üí   558‚Üí   558‚Üítraining session that takes your body beyond its current state, you‚Äôre applying
   486‚Üí   535‚Üí   559‚Üí   559‚Üía stress to your systems. The body initially responds to the stress in an alarm
   487‚Üí   536‚Üí   560‚Üí   560‚Üístage where fatigue is experienced possibly in the form of soreness or stiffness
   488‚Üí   537‚Üí   561‚Üí   561‚Üíin, generally, the first 24 to 48 hours following your training session. After this
   489‚Üí   538‚Üí   562‚Üí   562‚Üíalarm stage the body transitions into the resistance stage and the body further
   490‚Üí   539‚Üí   563‚Üí   563‚Üíadapts to the stress. In the final stage, the body experiences what is known as
   491‚Üí   540‚Üí   564‚Üí   564‚Üísupercompensation. Upon reaching the supercompensation stage, the body is at a
   492‚Üí   541‚Üí   565‚Üí   565‚Üíhigher level of adaptation which means you are becoming stronger or fitter than
   493‚Üí   542‚Üí   566‚Üí   566‚Üíyou were before the training.
